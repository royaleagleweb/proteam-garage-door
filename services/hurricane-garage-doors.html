Hurricane-Rated Garage Doors for South Florida

Your garage door is the largest and most vulnerable opening in your home during a hurricane. A standard door can fail under high winds, causing pressure changes that blow off roofs. ProTeam installs wind-rated and impact-resistant doors for South Florida's severe storms.

Florida Building Code & Wind Load Requirements

The Florida Building Code requires garage doors to meet specific wind load ratings based on location. In Broward and Palm Beach County, doors must withstand winds of 140 mph or higher. We ensure every installation meets or exceeds required design pressure ratings for your address, guaranteeing code compliance.

High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) Standards

Properties within the HVHZ face the strictest building requirements in the nation. Doors must carry a valid Miami-Dade Notice of Acceptance (NOA) confirming they passed large missile impact and cyclic pressure testing. We exclusively install NOA-approved products in these zones and provide all documentation for permit approval.

Wind-Rated Door Options

We carry hurricane-rated doors in every style, from raised panel to contemporary flush designs. Reinforcement includes internal bracing struts, heavier gauge steel, reinforced tracks, and impact-resistant glazing. Many wind-rated doors also feature polyurethane insulation that doubles as structural reinforcement while improving energy efficiency.

Insurance Discounts & Savings

Upgrading to a certified hurricane door can significantly reduce your insurance premium. Florida law requires insurers to offer wind mitigation discounts for approved opening protection. We provide a compliance certificate your agent can use to apply the discount immediately. Many homeowners offset a substantial portion of their investment through annual premium savings.
