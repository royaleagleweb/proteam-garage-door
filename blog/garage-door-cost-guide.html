How Much Does a New Garage Door Cost in 2026?

A transparent breakdown of garage door prices, installation fees, and the factors that shape your final investment.

Buying Guide March 3, 2026 8 min read ProTeam Garage Door

If you are shopping for a new garage door in Broward or Palm Beach County, your first question is almost certainly about cost. The answer depends on material, opening size, features, and whether your building code requires hurricane-rated construction. In this guide, we break down every line item on a garage door estimate in South Florida in 2026 so you can budget with confidence and avoid surprises.

Average Garage Door Prices by Material

The single biggest factor that determines the price of a new garage door is the material. Here in South Florida, steel, aluminum, and wood-composite doors dominate the market, though genuine wood and full-view glass doors have been gaining popularity in upscale communities like Weston, Parkland, and Palm Beach Gardens. Below is a quick overview of what each material typically costs for a standard single-car door, before installation.

Material Single Door (8x7) Double Door (16x7) Best For
Steel (non-insulated) $800 - $1,200 $1,100 - $1,800 Budget-friendly durability
Steel (insulated) $1,000 - $2,000 $1,500 - $3,000 Energy savings & quiet operation
Aluminum & Glass $1,500 - $3,000 $2,500 - $5,000+ Modern/contemporary homes
Wood Composite $1,200 - $2,500 $2,000 - $4,000 Wood look without maintenance
Real Wood $2,000 - $4,000 $3,500 - $5,000+ Custom curb appeal

As you can see, pricing ranges widely even within a single material category. The lower end of each range reflects a basic panel design with minimal extras, while the upper end includes premium paint finishes, decorative hardware, window inserts, and upgraded weatherstripping.

Single-Car vs. Double-Car Doors

A single-car garage door typically measures 8 to 9 feet wide and 7 feet tall. A double-car door spans 16 feet wide at the same height, though 8-foot-tall openings are increasingly common in newer South Florida construction. The jump from single to double is not simply double the price — hardware, springs, and reinforcement struts add proportionally more cost because a wider door requires heavier-duty components.

Many homeowners with two-car garages face another decision: one large 16-foot door or two separate 8-foot doors. Two singles generally cost 20 to 30 percent more than one double because you are purchasing two complete sets of tracks, springs, and hardware.

The Cost of Insulation

In South Florida's subtropical climate, insulation is not just about keeping cold air out during winter. An insulated garage door keeps intense summer heat from radiating into your home, which can meaningfully lower your air conditioning bill if your garage shares a wall with living space. Insulation also makes the door quieter and more rigid, which improves durability.

There are two common insulation types:

  • Polystyrene insulation is a rigid foam board placed inside the door panels. It offers R-values between 4 and 8, and typically adds $150 to $300 to the door's price.
  • Polyurethane insulation is injected as a liquid that expands and bonds to the steel skins. It delivers R-values of 12 to 18, provides superior sound dampening, and adds $300 to $600 to the cost.

For most South Florida homeowners, we recommend at least polystyrene insulation. If your garage is attached to your home or you use it as a workshop, polyurethane is worth the premium.

Garage Door Opener Costs

Your new garage door will need an opener system unless you plan to operate it manually. Opener prices have risen slightly in 2026 due to increased demand for smart-home-compatible models, but there are options for every budget.

  • Chain-drive openers are the most affordable at $200 to $350, though they tend to be noisier. They are a practical choice for detached garages where sound is not a concern.
  • Belt-drive openers range from $300 to $500 and operate almost silently. They are the most popular choice for attached garages throughout Broward County.
  • Wall-mount (jackshaft) openers cost $400 to $700 and free up ceiling space entirely. They are ideal for garages with high or cathedral ceilings.
  • Smart openers with Wi-Fi such as the LiftMaster myQ series add $50 to $150 on top of the base unit, giving you app-based control, real-time alerts, and voice-assistant integration.

Professional opener installation typically costs $150 to $250 in labor, which covers mounting the unit, wiring, programming remotes, and adjusting travel limits and force settings for safe operation.

Installation Labor Costs

Labor is the second largest component of your total project cost after the door itself. In the Broward and Palm Beach County market, you can expect to pay the following for professional installation:

  • Standard installation (single door, existing tracks): $300 to $500
  • Standard installation (double door, existing tracks): $400 to $700
  • New construction or frame modification: $600 to $1,200, depending on the scope of structural work required

Labor covers anchoring the tracks, mounting the spring system, hanging and aligning panels, installing weatherstripping, connecting the opener, and performing a safety check. A quality crew can finish a straightforward replacement in three to four hours.

Old Door Removal and Disposal Fees

If you are replacing an existing door, the old one needs to come down first. Most installers charge a removal and disposal fee of $75 to $200 per door, covering panel detachment, safe spring unwinding, track dismantling, and hauling to a recycling center.

Some companies include removal in their installation quote, so always ask whether it is bundled or listed as a separate line item. At ProTeam Garage Door, we include old door removal with every full installation at no extra charge.

Hurricane-Rating Premium in South Florida

This is the cost factor that surprises many homeowners who are new to Florida. The Florida Building Code requires garage doors in our region to withstand specific wind-load pressures, and both Broward and Palm Beach County sit within the High-Velocity Hurricane Zone. That means your new door must meet strict impact-resistance and wind-pressure standards, which adds to the base price.

Expect a hurricane-rating premium of $300 to $1,000 on top of the standard door price, depending on the door's size and the specific wind-speed zone your home falls within. This premium covers reinforced horizontal and vertical bracing, thicker gauge steel, impact-rated glazing if the door has windows, and a heavier-duty track and spring system.

While the upfront cost is higher, a hurricane-rated garage door delivers significant long-term value. It can qualify you for wind mitigation insurance discounts that often save homeowners $500 to $1,500 per year on their property insurance, meaning the upgrade can pay for itself within the first year or two.

Other Factors That Affect Your Total Price

Beyond the core components discussed above, several additional variables can push your project cost up or down:

Door Design and Style

A basic raised-panel door is the most affordable option. Upgrading to a carriage-house style, flush panel, or custom design adds $200 to $800 to the door price. Window inserts, decorative hardware kits, and premium paint or stain finishes each carry their own surcharges as well.

Custom Sizing

Standard openings that match manufacturer stock sizes are the most cost-effective. If your garage has a non-standard width or height, you may need a custom-built door, which can add 20 to 40 percent to the base price and extend lead times by two to four weeks.

Permits and Inspections

In Broward and Palm Beach County, a building permit is typically required for new garage door installations. Permit fees range from $75 to $250, and an inspection may follow to verify code compliance. Your installer should handle the permit process on your behalf.

Existing Structural Issues

If the opening header is rotted, the framing is out of square, or the concrete slab has settled, corrective work will be needed before the new door can be installed. These repairs can add $200 to $1,000+ to the project, depending on severity.

Warranty Coverage

Most manufacturers offer a limited lifetime warranty on sections and a one- to five-year warranty on hardware and springs. Extended packages cost $100 to $300.

How to Get the Best Value on Your New Garage Door

Price matters, but it should never be the only factor driving your decision. Here are some practical tips to maximize value without sacrificing quality:

  • Get at least three written estimates from licensed, insured contractors. Compare not just the total but the individual line items, warranty terms, and what is included versus excluded.
  • Ask about manufacturer rebates and seasonal promotions. Brands like Clopay, Amarr, and C.H.I. periodically offer cash-back incentives that your installer can pass along.
  • Consider long-term costs, not just upfront price. A slightly more expensive insulated, hurricane-rated door can save you hundreds per year on energy bills and insurance premiums.
  • Check financing options. Many reputable companies, including ProTeam Garage Door, offer flexible payment plans that let you spread the cost over time with low or zero interest.
  • Do not skip the permit. Unpermitted work can create costly problems when you sell your home or file an insurance claim.
