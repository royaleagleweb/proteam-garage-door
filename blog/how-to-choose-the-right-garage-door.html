How to Choose the Right Garage Door for Your South Florida Home

Materials, styles, insulation, hurricane ratings, and budget considerations for Broward and Palm Beach County homeowners.

Buying Guide March 15, 2026 8 min read ProTeam Garage Door

Your garage door accounts for roughly 30 percent of your home's front facade, making it one of the most visible and impactful features of your property. In South Florida, the decision carries even more weight because every new door must stand up to intense heat, salt air, driving rain, and the ever-present threat of hurricanes. Choosing the wrong material or skipping critical features can cost you thousands of dollars in repairs, insurance premiums, and lost energy efficiency over the life of the door.

This guide walks you through every factor you need to consider before buying a new garage door in Broward or Palm Beach County, from materials and styles to insulation values and hurricane ratings. By the time you finish reading, you will have a clear roadmap for selecting the best door for your home, your climate, and your budget.

Step 1: Choose Your Material

The material you select determines not only how your garage door looks but also how it performs over the next 20 to 30 years. South Florida's subtropical environment creates unique demands that make some materials far more practical than others. Here is a detailed look at each option.

Steel Garage Doors

Steel is the most popular garage door material in the United States, and for good reason. Modern steel doors feature a galvanized core with a baked-on polyester or vinyl finish that resists rust, fading, and chipping. They are available in single-layer, double-layer, and triple-layer construction, with the latter offering the best insulation and noise reduction. In South Florida, steel doors pair well with our climate as long as they carry a proper factory finish rated for coastal exposure. Expect steel doors to last 20 to 30 years with minimal maintenance.

Price range for a standard 16x7 double door: $1,100 to $3,000, depending on insulation level and panel design.

Aluminum Garage Doors

Aluminum is the go-to material for homeowners who want a sleek, contemporary look. Full-view aluminum-and-glass doors have surged in popularity throughout communities like Boca Raton, Weston, and Palm Beach Gardens, where modern architecture is prevalent. Aluminum naturally resists corrosion, which makes it an excellent choice for coastal properties within a few miles of the ocean. However, aluminum is softer than steel and more prone to denting, so it may not be the best pick if your driveway sees heavy activity or you have children who play near the garage.

Price range for a standard 16x7 double door: $2,500 to $5,000+, with tempered or impact-rated glass panels at the higher end.

Wood Garage Doors

Wood delivers a warmth and character that no other material can replicate. Cedar, redwood, and mahogany are the most common species used for garage doors, and each offers a distinct grain pattern and natural beauty. The downside in South Florida is maintenance. Wood expands and contracts with our extreme humidity swings, and without regular sealing or staining every one to two years, it can warp, crack, or develop mold. If you love the look of wood but prefer lower upkeep, consider a wood-composite or faux-wood steel door that mimics the grain without the maintenance burden.

Price range for a standard 16x7 double door: $3,500 to $5,000+ for genuine wood; $2,000 to $4,000 for wood composite.

Fiberglass Garage Doors

Fiberglass is a niche option that deserves consideration in coastal South Florida. It is naturally resistant to salt air corrosion, it will not rust, and it does not expand or contract the way wood does. Fiberglass doors can be manufactured to mimic the appearance of wood grain, giving you the aesthetic benefits without the high maintenance. On the downside, fiberglass can become brittle over time with prolonged UV exposure, and it offers less impact resistance than steel or aluminum. It is a solid choice for properties within a few blocks of the beach where salt spray is a constant concern.

Price range for a standard 16x7 double door: $1,500 to $3,500.

Step 2: Select a Style That Fits Your Home

Garage door styles have evolved far beyond the plain raised-panel design of decades past. Today you can choose from a wide variety of looks that complement virtually any architectural style found in South Florida.

  • Raised panel remains the most common and affordable style. It works well with traditional, colonial, and ranch-style homes and is available in short, long, and recessed panel configurations.
  • Carriage house doors mimic the look of historic swing-out barn doors while operating on a modern overhead track system. They are extremely popular in Mediterranean and Spanish-style homes throughout Coral Springs, Plantation, and Wellington.
  • Flush panel doors offer a clean, minimalist look that pairs beautifully with contemporary and mid-century modern homes. Many flush-panel models can be ordered with horizontal grooves or subtle texture for added visual interest.
  • Full-view glass doors feature aluminum frames with large glass panels, creating a dramatic modern statement. They are ideal for homes with front-facing garages that double as entertaining spaces or workshops.
  • Custom designs let you mix materials, panel configurations, window shapes, and decorative hardware to create a one-of-a-kind door that perfectly matches your vision.

Step 3: Understand Insulation and R-Values

Insulation might seem like an afterthought in a warm climate, but it plays a critical role in South Florida for several reasons. An insulated garage door keeps the brutal summer heat from radiating through the garage wall into your air-conditioned living space. It also dampens street noise, adds structural rigidity to the door panels, and reduces vibration during opening and closing cycles.

R-value measures a material's thermal resistance. The higher the R-value, the better the insulation. Here is how the common options stack up:

  • Non-insulated (single layer): R-value of 0. The door is just a single sheet of steel or aluminum with no thermal barrier. Only suitable for detached garages that are not climate controlled.
  • Polystyrene insulation (double layer): R-value of 4 to 8. A rigid foam board is placed between the exterior steel skin and an interior backer. This is a cost-effective upgrade that provides moderate thermal protection.
  • Polyurethane insulation (triple layer): R-value of 12 to 18. Liquid foam is injected between two steel skins, expanding to fill every cavity. This is the gold standard for insulation, offering the highest R-values, the best noise reduction, and the greatest panel strength.

For most homeowners in Broward and Palm Beach County with an attached garage, we recommend a polyurethane-insulated door with an R-value of at least 12. The upfront cost premium of $300 to $600 typically pays for itself within a few years through reduced cooling costs.

Step 4: Hurricane Ratings Are Non-Negotiable

If you live anywhere in South Florida, your new garage door must meet the Florida Building Code requirements for wind resistance. Both Broward and Palm Beach County fall within the High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ), which mandates some of the strictest building standards in the nation. A non-rated garage door cannot legally be installed in these areas, and it would leave your home dangerously vulnerable during a storm.

When a garage door fails during a hurricane, wind pressure floods the interior of the home, pushing upward on the roof structure and outward on the walls. This pressure imbalance is one of the leading causes of catastrophic structural failure during major storms. A properly rated door prevents that scenario.

Key specifications to look for include:

  • Wind load rating: Measured in pounds per square foot (psf). Most South Florida homes require doors rated for at least 140 mph winds, which translates to a design pressure of roughly 30 to 50 psf depending on door size and location.
  • Large missile impact testing: The door must withstand a 9-pound 2x4 lumber projectile fired at 34 mph, simulating wind-borne debris during a Category 4 or 5 hurricane.
  • Miami-Dade NOA (Notice of Acceptance): The gold standard of product approval. If a door carries a Miami-Dade NOA, it meets or exceeds every wind and impact requirement in the state.

A hurricane-rated door typically adds $300 to $1,000 to the base price, but the investment frequently qualifies you for significant wind mitigation insurance discounts under Florida Statute 627.0629, often saving $500 to $1,500 per year on your homeowner's policy.

Step 5: Get the Right Size

Standard residential garage door sizes in South Florida are 8x7, 9x7, and 16x7 feet (width by height). However, newer construction increasingly features 8-foot-tall openings to accommodate larger SUVs and trucks. Before ordering, you or your installer must take precise measurements of the opening width, opening height, headroom (the space between the top of the opening and the ceiling), side room, and backroom (the depth of the garage from the opening to the back wall).

If your garage has a non-standard opening, you may need a custom-built door. Custom sizing typically adds 20 to 40 percent to the base price and extends lead times by two to four weeks. Your installer should verify all measurements during the on-site consultation to avoid costly errors.

Step 6: Set a Realistic Budget

Garage door projects in South Florida span a wide range depending on material, size, insulation, and hurricane requirements. Here are general budget ranges for a complete installation including the door, hardware, opener, labor, and hurricane compliance:

  • Economy ($1,800 to $2,500): Non-insulated or lightly insulated steel door with a chain-drive opener. Suitable for rental properties or budget-conscious homeowners.
  • Mid-range ($3,000 to $5,000): Insulated steel or wood-composite door with windows, a belt-drive smart opener, and full hurricane rating. This is the sweet spot for most homeowners in Broward and Palm Beach County.
  • Premium ($5,000 to $8,000+): Full-view aluminum-and-glass, custom wood, or designer steel door with a wall-mount smart opener, impact-rated glazing, and premium finishes. Ideal for luxury homes and those seeking maximum curb appeal.

Remember that a new garage door offers one of the highest returns on investment of any home improvement project. According to the 2025 Cost vs. Value Report by Remodeling Magazine, a new garage door recoups over 90 percent of its cost at resale, and in many markets the return exceeds 100 percent.

Step 7: Choose a Trusted Brand

Not all garage doors are created equal. The manufacturer you choose determines the quality of the steel, the precision of the engineering, and the strength of the warranty. Here are the brands we recommend and install most frequently in South Florida.

Clopay

The largest residential garage door manufacturer in North America, Clopay offers the widest selection of styles, colors, and materials. Their Canyon Ridge collection delivers stunning faux-wood looks, while the Avante series leads the full-view glass market. Clopay doors carry a limited lifetime warranty on sections and are available with Miami-Dade NOA approval.

Amarr

Amarr is known for exceptional build quality and a streamlined ordering process. Their Stratford and Hillcrest collections cover the full range from budget-friendly raised panel to premium carriage house designs. Amarr offers SafeGuard wind-load reinforcement as a factory-installed option, making hurricane compliance seamless.

Wayne Dalton

Wayne Dalton is an innovator in the garage door space. They pioneered the TorqueMaster spring system, which encloses torsion springs inside a steel tube for added safety. Their Classic Steel and Designer Fiberglass lines are popular in South Florida, and their WindStorm protection system meets the highest Florida Building Code requirements.

Making Your Final Decision

Choosing the right garage door comes down to balancing aesthetics, performance, and budget within the unique demands of the South Florida climate. Start by selecting a material that suits your home's style and your maintenance tolerance. Then ensure the door is properly insulated and carries the hurricane rating required by your local building code. Finally, choose a reputable brand backed by a strong warranty and a trusted local installer who will handle permits, measurements, and professional installation.
